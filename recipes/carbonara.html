Spaghetti alla Carbonara

Description

Carbonara is a Roman dish made with eggs, hard cheese, and cured pork.
Its signature rich and silky sauce comes from beaten eggs tossed with hot
pasta. The trick to making carbonara is making sure the pasta is hot enough
to cook the eggs, but not so hot that they curdle.

Ingredients

  • 2 tsp olive oil
  • Guanciale, diced
  • 1 pack Spaghetti
  • 3 large Eggs
  • 10 tbsps Pecorino Romanno Cheese
  • Salt
  • Pepper

Steps

  1. Cook the pork in olive oil until browned and crispy, then drain on
    paper towels.
  2. Boil the spaghetti in salted water. Drain and return to the pot.
    Let cool.
  3. Pour the egg mixture over the pasta, stirring quickly, until creamy.
  4. Stir in the pork, then top with the remaining cheese and more black
    pepper.
